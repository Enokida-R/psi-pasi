Primates

  • 1.chinmapnzee
    We humans are members of a group of monkeys known as primates. In the past, humans and chimpanzees were one and the same species. About five million years ago, they branched off to become humans and chimpanzees as we know them today.
  • 2.gorilla
    General name for mammals classified in the primate order Hominidae (gorillas). Distributed in central Africa. The largest great apes, reaching 120-180 cm in length and 200 kg in weight. The population numbers 100,000-200,000 individuals.
  • 3.orangutan
    The orangutan (Malay for 'man of the forest') is the only large great ape in Asia.It is thought that long ago they were widely distributed throughout the islands of South-East Asia and Indochina, but today they are found only in the tropical forests of Borneo (Kalimantan) and Sumatra.
  • 4.human
    Human is broadly a generic term for animals belonging to the subfamily Homininae[1] and narrowly refers to living (currently inhabited) humans (scientific name : Homo sapiens), Homo sapiens sapiens, Homo sapiens sapiens sapiens), Homo sapiens sapiens.
